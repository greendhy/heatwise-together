    Project shape

    Built for a small public pilot

    Live

    Runs in the browser and can read current public weather data by city.

    Open

    Designed for no-login public use, easy copying, and low-bandwidth access.

    Clear

    Turns a risk notice into three actions and one care check-in.

    No

    No medical advice, private tracking, paid ads, or business checkout.

    The first release is intentionally small: a working card generator, a short public template library, and a simple way for caregivers or volunteers to share a safer hot-day plan.

    Reusable public assets

    Open climate cards

    Each card can be copied into WeChat, email, SMS, a community website, or a senior center notice. The public version will be free, low-bandwidth, and usable without logging in.

    Why it matters

    Climate information is often too hard to act on

    Problem

    Alerts are written for agencies, not for an older person deciding what to do in the next three hours.

    Intervention

    Convert the alert into three immediate actions, one care check, and a shareable large-type card.

    Public benefit

    Families, volunteers, and local groups can reuse the cards before heat, smoke, rain, or outage events.

    Four-week online launch

    Built to be live quickly

    1. Week 1 Finish the public card generator, static hosting, privacy page, and bilingual templates.
    2. Week 2 Test cards with older users, caregivers, and accessibility reviewers; improve large-type and screen-reader flow.
    3. Week 3 Add public card library, copy/share workflow, analytics-free feedback form, and backup hosting.
    4. Week 4 Publish the free site, release the templates, document the pilot, and invite community reuse.
